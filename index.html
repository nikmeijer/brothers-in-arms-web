    About the Memorial

    "Over a million Indian soldiers served during World War I, often alongside Canadian troops, in crucial battles across Europe. At places like Ypres and the Somme, Indian and Canadian soldiers fought side by side, strengthening the Allied forces and demonstrating unity in the face of adversity. The Brothers in Arms Memorial commemorates their shared history and sacrifices."

    Letters from the Soldiers

    Learn their story and get to know them.

    “Tell my mother not to go wandering madly because her son, my brother, is dead. To be born and to die is God's order. Some day we must die, sooner or later, and if I die here, who will remember me? It is a fine thing to die far from home. A saint said this, and, as he was a good man, it must be true.”

    A wounded Sikh soldier for the British-Indian army,
    18 January, 1915
